# Business_Case_Study_BS-SQL
1. Analyzed 100K+ orders (2016–2018) across 4,119 cities and 27 states, uncovering large-scale operational and customer trends.

2. Identified exponential order growth (329 → 45K → 54K) and 136.98% increase in order value (2017–2018), indicating rapid e-commerce expansion.

3. Detected strong seasonality, with peak demand in Nov & Jan, and highest activity during afternoon hours (38K+ orders).

4. Found high customer concentration (42%) in a single state (SP), highlighting regional dependency and expansion opportunities.

5. Revealed significant logistics cost variation, with remote states showing highest average freight costs, indicating last-mile inefficiencies.

6. Identified delivery performance gaps, with certain states facing longer delivery times, while urban regions achieved fastest fulfillment.

7. Observed early vs delayed deliveries, highlighting scope to optimize delivery time predictions and improve customer satisfaction.

8. Determined credit card as dominant payment mode and single-installment preference, indicating low-friction payment behavior.

9. Recommended regional warehouse expansion, demand forecasting, and targeted market strategies, enabling cost optimization and revenue growth.
